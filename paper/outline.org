* Abstract
- what markov disccovery is
- why markov blanket discovery is useful
- however hard problem, large search space
- previous approaches avoid this large search space
- but with the trade off of not being able to capture
- certain higher order relationship between variables.
- most notably, as far as we are aware, existing methods
- are not capable of consistently discovering a simple xor relationship
  between just 3 variables
- Therefore in this paper, we revisit the possibility of exact markov
  blanket discovery 
- By modifying the existing IAMB method for markov blanket
  discovery resulting in our approach called LIAM-MB.
- Specifically we will additional step after the forward selection phase
  of IAMB
- where we will use techniques common in frequent itemset mining to
  search the subset lattice of all possible expansions of the current
  candidate markov blanket from phase 1.
- Experiments on synthetic Bayesian networks containing simple xor and
  parity relationships shows that LIAM-MB is able to capture those
  relationships while other approaches are unable to.
- Of course, the exact nature of LIAM-MB comes at a cost; further
  experiments on common benchmark datasets will show how LIAM-MB
  compares to existing Markov blanket discovery approaches
- Further experiments on common benchmark datasets will show the cost of
  the exact nature of LIAM-MB, paticularly when compared to IAMB
